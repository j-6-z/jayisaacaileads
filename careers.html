Our Principles

Five things we actually believe.

Not posters on a wall. These are how we make decisions when it gets hard.

01 / SPEED
Ship daily. Iterate constantly.
We'd rather ship 50% of an idea today and learn from real users than spend a quarter perfecting something nobody asked for. Big companies move slow because they have to. We don't.
02 / HONESTY
Say the real thing.
To each other, to customers, to investors. Sales theatre is the enemy. If a feature isn't working, we say so. If a customer is asking for something we can't build, we tell them. Direct > comfortable.
03 / CUSTOMERS
Talk to users. Constantly.
Every person on the team — engineering, design, ops — talks to customers regularly. Not just sales. The closer everyone is to the user, the better the product gets.
04 / OWNERSHIP
No "that's not my job."
Everyone here is expected to own outcomes, not tasks. See a bug? Fix it. See an unhappy customer? Reach out. See an opportunity? Pursue it. We hire people who don't wait for permission.
05 / STAY SMALL, STAY SHARP
Hire slowly. Pay well. Stay lean.
Bigger isn't better. We'd rather have 12 brilliant people than 60 average ones. Every hire has to clear a high bar. In exchange, we pay top of market, give serious equity, and trust people to do their job without micromanagement.
How We Work

No theatre. No meetings about meetings. Just work.

Remote-first, North America
Work from wherever in Canada or the US. We sync async-first, meet only when necessary, and run on Slack + Linear. HQ is Saskatoon, but you don't need to be.
Your hours, your output
We don't track time. We track shipped work. Want to take Wednesday afternoon to take your kid to the dentist? Cool. Want to start your day at noon? Cool. Just deliver.
Minimal process
No stand-ups. No status meetings. No quarterly OKR theatre. We have a roadmap, a shared sense of urgency, and trust. If you need 6 layers of process to do your job, this isn't the team for you.
Real ownership
If you own a project, you actually own it — decision authority included. We hire smart people and get out of their way. No PMs writing tickets for engineers.
Tools you actually want
Cursor, Linear, Vercel, Firebase, Stripe, Slack. Modern stack, no legacy enterprise software, no IT department gatekeeping which apps you can install.
Ship the product, not the company
Brag-deck SaaS metrics are easy. Building software people actually use is hard. We focus on the second one. Your work matters because customers feel it — not because it ladders to a board slide.
